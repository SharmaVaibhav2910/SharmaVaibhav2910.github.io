  2. The Idea of Satyagraha
    3. 

      

    1. Mahatma Gandhi returned to India in January, 1915. His heroic fight for the Indians in South Africa was well-known. 
His novel method of mass agitation known as Satyagraha had yielded good results.
      2. 

    2. The idea of Satyagraha emphasized the power of truth and the need to search for truth.
      3. 

    3. In 1916, Gandhi travelled to Champaran in Bihar to inspire the peasants to struggle against the oppressive plantation 
system.
      4. 

    4. In 1917,crops field in Kheda district of Gujrat, but the government refused to remit land revenue and insisted on its 
full collection.
      5. 

    5. In 1918, Mahatma Gandhi intervened in a dispute between workers and mill owners of Ahmedabad. He advised to 
workers to go on strike and to demand a 35% increase in wages.
      6. 

    6. Satyagraha brought Gandhiji into close touch with the workers in the urban areas.
      7. 

    


  3. The Rowlatt act
    4. 

      

    1. When the Rawlatt act 1919, was passed hurriedly through the Imperial Legislative Council inspire of unanimous opposition 
of the Indian members, Gandhiji’s patience comes to an end.
      2. 

    2. Gandhi wanted non-violent civil disobedience against such unjust laws, which would start with a hartal on 6th April.
      3. 

    3. 6th April 1919 was observed as Satyagraha Day when people all over the country observed fast and hartal.
      4. 

    4. 1919, the country witnessed a remarkable political awakening in India.
      5. 

    5. Local leaders were picked up from Amritsar and Mahatma Gandhi was barred from entering Delhi.
      6. 

    6. On 10th April, the police in Amritsar fired upon a peaceful procession, provoking widespread attacks on banks.
      7. 

    


  4. Jallianwala Bagh Massacre
    5. 

      

    1. A large crowd gathered in the enclosed ground of Jalliawalla Bagh.
      2. 

    2. People came to protest against government’s repressive measure while some came to attend the annual Baisakhi fair.
      3. 

    3. General Dyer entered the area. Blocked the exit points and opened fire on the crowd, killing hundreds.
      4. 

    4. The government responded with brutal repression seeking to humiliate and terrorise people.
      5. 

    5. Satyagrahis were forced to rub their noses on the ground, crawl on the streets and do Salaam (salute) to all Sahibs.
      6. 

    


  5. Khilafat movement
    6. 

      

    1. Rowlatt Satyagraha had been a widespread movement, it was still limited mostly to cities and towns.
      2. 

    2. Mahatma Gandhi now felt the need to launch a more broad based movement in India.
      3. 

    3. But he was certain that no such movement could be organized without bringing the Hindus and Muslims closer together.
      4. 

    4. The First World War had ended with the defeat of Ottoman Turkey. There were rumors that a harsh peace treaty was going 
to be imposed on the Ottoman Emperor, who was the spiritual head (Khalifa) of the Islamic world.
      5. 

    5. The Muslims of India decided to force Britain to change her Turkish policy.
      6. 

    6. A Khalifa Committee was formed under the leadership of Maulana Azad, Ajmal Khan and Hasrat Mohani.
      7. 

    7. A young generation of Muslim leaders like the brothers Muhammad Ali and Shaukat Ali began discussing with Mahatma 
Gandhi about the possibility of a united mass action on the issue.
      8. 

    


  6. Differing strands within the movement:
    7. 

      

    1. Rebellion in the countryside: – From the cities, the noncooperation movement spread to the countryside. After the war, 
the struggles of peasants and tribal were developing in different parts of India.
      2. 

    2. One movement here war against talukdars and landlords who demanded from peasant exorbitantly high rents and a 
variety of other cesses.
      3. 

    3. Peasants had to do begar. The peasant movement demanded reduction of revenue, an abolition of begar and social boycott 
of oppressive landlords.
      4. 

    4. Oudh Kisan Sabha was setup headed by. Jawaharlal Nehru and other, within a month, over 300 branches had been set up 
by the villagers.
      5. 

    5. Tribal peasants interpreted the message of Mahatma Gandhi and the idea of Swaraj in yet another way.
      6. 

    6. The colonial government had closed large forest areas preventing people from entering the forests to graze their cattle, or 
to collect fuel wood and fruits.
      7. 

    7. Alluri Sitaram Raju Claimed that he had a variety of special powers. He asserted that India could be liberated only by the use 
of force.

  8. The Sense of Collective Belonging
    9. 

      

    1. Nationalist Movement Spreads when people belonging to different regions and communities begin to develop a sense of 
collective belongingness. The identity of a nation is most often symbolized in a figure or image.
      2. 

    2. This image of Bharat Mata was first created by Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay in 1870 when he wrote ‘Vande Mataram ‘ 
for our motherland. Indian folk songs and folk sung by bards played an important role in making the idea of nationalism. In 
Bengal, Rabindranath Tagore and in Madras, Natesa, Sastri collection of folk tales and songs, which led the movement for 
folk revival.
      3. 

    3. During the Swadeshi Movement, a tri-color ( red, green and yellow ) flag was designed in Bengal. It had eight lotuses 
representing eight provinces and a crescent moon representing Hindus and Muslims.
      4. 

    4. Means of creating a feeling of nationalism was through reinterpretation of history. The nationalist writers urged the 
readers to take pride in India’s great achievements in the past and struggle to change the miserable conditions of life 
under British rule.
